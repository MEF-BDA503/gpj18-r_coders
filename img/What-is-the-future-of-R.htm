Anurag Pandey, Experience in Machine Learning Projects with R and Hadoop
Answered Apr 9

R is now considered as the most popular analytic tool in the world. Estimates of some users range from 250000 to over 2 million. As per my experience I will tell you the future scope of R Programming Language, Career growth , various job roles in R and list of companies that use R for analytics.

Let us discuss one by one-

R is the hands-down winner if we will look at online popularity. It has more blogs, discussion groups, and email lists than any other tool including SAS. Thus, R was again the top choice in most of the surveys.

R has been a game changer in the analytics industry

Over time, the core team expanded and thousands of people from around the globe have contributed to making R the way it is now.

R is not the first open-source analytics tool. There were many others before it. Hence, Most of the earlier open-source tools struggled with scalability.

Best Big Data Analytics Tools for 2018 -This article will help you to learn more about data analytics tools

What about job prospects?

Career in R programming offers bright job prospects for any data scientist novice or experienced.

Indian companies are increasingly looking at R. Genpact, Accenture and Wipro are encouraging their staff to build expertise on R and associated technologies.

At the same time, organizations expect many of the new hires to already equipped with knowledge of R. They want them to be familiar with the R tool and how to use it for data analysis.

Business Adoption of R

Most of the data scientist and analysts use R as a tool of their choice. But what about corporate adoption?

Large Businesses use open-source tools and technologies for a business process that is as critical as data analysis. But, R has slowly won over the hearts of many large corporates.

Companies like Google, Facebook, Genpact, Accenture, MuSigma and many others are adopting the R platform.

Job Roles in R

R jobs are not only being offered by IT companies but all types of companies are hiring High paid R candidates including-

  • Financial firms
  • Retail organizations
  • Banks
  • Healthcare organizations etc.

There is a huge demand for R programmers among start-ups. Companies have several R job openings with various positions like:

  • R data scientist
  • Data scientist(IT)
  • Analyst manager
  • Senior data analyst
  • Business analyst
  • Analyst consultant

The Data Analyst

Language R is the part of the data analysts basic knowledge. Data analyst role in R language career, which combines technical and analytical knowledge with ingenuity.

Roles:

Collects, processes and perform statistical data analysis.

Skills needed to become Data Analyst:

  • Spreadsheet tool(eg. Excel)
  • Communication and Visualization
  • Database System
  • Math, stats, Machine Learning

b) The Data Architect

More and more organizations are in need of a data architect. Industries like banking and FMCG use data architects. They use to integrate and maintain their data sources. They often work with the latest technologies such as Spark and always need to be on top of the game to stay relevant.

Role:

Create BluePrints for data management system.

Skills needed to become Data Architect:

  • Data Warehousing Solutions
  • In-depth knowledge of database architecture.
  • Extract transformation and load, spreadsheet and BI tools.
  • Data Modeling
  • System Development

The Database Administrator

You must ensure that the database is accessible to every stakeholder in the organizations.

You need to master different technologies going from SQL and XML up to a more general programming language like Java.

Role:

Ensures that the database is available to all relevant users, is performing properly and is being kept safe.

Skills needed to become Database Administrator:

  • Backup and Recovery
  • Data modeling and design.
  • Distributed computing
  • Database System
  • Data Security
  • Business knowledge
